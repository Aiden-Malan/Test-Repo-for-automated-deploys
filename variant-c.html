Auditing Services

An audit is an independent examination of your financial statements to ensure accuracy and compliance with International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS). Our audits provide stakeholders with confidence in your financial reporting and help identify areas for improvement in your internal controls.

Tax Compliance

B-BBEE Verification

Broad-Based Black Economic Empowerment (B-BBEE) is a South African government initiative to redress apartheid inequalities. B-BBEE verification assesses your company's contribution and issues a certificate that enhances your ability to do business with government and large corporations. We guide you through the entire verification process.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our accounting services answered

How often should my business undergo an audit?

The frequency of audits depends on your company size, industry regulations, and stakeholder requirements. Public companies require annual audits by law. Private companies may need audits if they exceed certain thresholds or if required by shareholders, lenders, or investors. We recommend discussing your specific situation with us to determine the appropriate audit schedule for your business.

What documents do I need for tax preparation?

For comprehensive tax preparation, you'll need: income statements, expense receipts, bank statements, previous year's tax returns, payroll records (if applicable), VAT records, asset purchase invoices, and any investment income documentation. We provide a detailed checklist tailored to your business type and can help you organize these documents efficiently.

How does B-BBEE certification benefit my business?

B-BBEE certification enhances your business's competitiveness when tendering for government contracts and doing business with large corporations that have B-BBEE procurement targets. A good B-BBEE rating can also improve your brand reputation, attract investment, and demonstrate your commitment to transformation and economic inclusion in South Africa.

What's the difference between bookkeeping and accounting?

Bookkeeping involves recording daily financial transactions systematically. Accounting goes further by analyzing, interpreting, and reporting financial data to help you make business decisions. While bookkeeping is transactional, accounting is analytical and strategic. MFH provides both services to ensure your financial records are accurate and actionable.

How can I reduce my business tax liability legally?

Legal tax reduction strategies include: maximizing allowable deductions, utilizing tax incentives and allowances, proper timing of income and expenses, retirement fund contributions, structuring your business efficiently, and taking advantage of small business corporation tax rates if eligible. Our tax specialists can develop a customized tax planning strategy for your specific circumstances.
